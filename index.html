About Me

On the business side:

I'm a software engineer with a background in IT resource management. In 2019 I successfully transitioned from hardware to software when I completed General Assembly Boston's Software Engineering Immersive  program. I'm a habitual learner, dedicated to living the maxim of "learn something new every day", which partly manifests itself through a ravenous consumption of industry newsletters. I've held a variety of positions over the years, all of which have shaped me into someone who is perpetually curious and always seeking to learn and grow.

I was most recently employed at HqO, Inc. as an App Release Engineer, with my primary role being to organize and automate the release process for HqO's apps. During my tenure, we scaled our release process from handling 50 whitelabels to over 170. More than just releasing new versions of existing apps, I also launched a fair number myself. If you want a more granular view, take a peek at my LinkedIn page.

I've been on the receiving end of some truly extraordinary mentorship and friendship over the years. I believe in paying it forward, and would be more than happy to lend a hand if I'm able. Please feel free to reach out if you need someone to look over some code, or have questions about best practices in building websites. If I don't have the answers, I can leverage my network to find someone who might. Software is a remarkably helpful community, and we all benefit from this cooperation.

Newsletters worth checking out: A11y Weekly , Frontend Focus , JAMstacked , JavaScript Weekly , Responsive Web Design & UserInterfacing

On the personal side:

I have a passion for travel, and have been to 13 countries and 22 US States. I also enjoy reading--mostly fantasy and science-fiction--with an annual goal of at least 45 books. I am a self-taught drummer who jams to blues, blues-rock, and rock. Not surprisingly, my musical tastes extend far beyond my skills behind the kit. My favorite sports team is AS Roma, who compete in Serie A--the Italian soccer league--and I try to watch every match I can. As an alumnus of Clemson University, I would be remiss if I didn't include my exuberance for Clemson Football; you can catch me leading the cheers at the New England Clemson Club's favorite watering hole during the season.

Projects

Active focus:

I just wrapped up version 3.0 of my Recipe Search app! I expanded functionality so users can click "View More" to reveal additional recipes from their search. It's a simple enough feature, which of course means it was far more difficult to implement. Give it a go!

In addition to that, I'm exicted to learn D3! With my affinity for reading and for self-analysis, it seems like this would be a great entry point into learning data visualization. I've written about this on LinkedIn. I've already completed some coursework on freeCodeCamp to get the basics under me, and I should have some of their projects done soon as well. Once I've gotten a handle on it, I can build something fancy for myself!

Of course, my learning is never complete. There are things about JavaScript (my primary coding language) that I 1) don't know, 2) don't know well enough, or 3) once knew but have since forgotten. In order to shore up my knowledge and fill in any gaps, I've been working through freeCodeCamp's JavaScript Algorithms and Data Structures course. Doing this is designed to strengthen my engineering foundation, while I use the newsletters referenced above to build the upper stories of my engineering stack.
